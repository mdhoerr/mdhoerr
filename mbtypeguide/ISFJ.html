ISFJ Home Pages 
Lum's World 
(added 10 May 2000)   
No, her name isn't Lum. Jennifer's site it devoted to fans of the Anime character Lum. It includes her own original graphics. There is an incredible amount of beautiful information about this topic. The site is beautifully laid out and what I surfed through worked perfectly. In fact, it is probably the most attractive and technically savvy (I don't mean leading edge - just that appropriate technologies are used appropriately) functional home page I have in the sections on home pages by type.
Shelagh's Guide to the World Wide Web   
(added 12 Apr 1999)   
The first page has a table of contents, like a book. The "chapters" take you to various pages that both tell you a little about Shelagh, and hopefully also show you something useful: books, music, drama, British comedy, and so on. And at the botttom of the page, she says email me, "but only if you really want to."  
Jill Sharp's Little Place On The Web   
(added 22 Mar 1999, updated 10 May 2000)   
As of 10 May 2000, this site was no longer available.
Her home page starts with a photo linking to Share International, then a tribute to AA, and to her sister. Next on the page is contact information: sign/view guestbook, description of the other pages (Links are on page 2, NY Giants are on page 3 and Awards are on page 4), cute picture with email info and ICQ info. At the bottom of the page is a family photo and animated footballs. Each page has different music, and each page has guestbook, email info, a family photo and animated footballs at the bottom of the page.  
Brandon Campbell's Web Playground   
(added 16 August 1999)  
used to be called Brandon's Web Tundra, and before that was Brandon's Web Island. It starts with a picture of him on a playground slide, then a list of links giving his vital statistics: Birth date and place, city where he live, occupation, Denton, Texas, height, weight, astrological sign, and Myers-Briggs personality type. Then you get links to his three favorite search engines. If you check out the archived stuff from his previous site, you find his interests in the "Three C's": Computers, Cars and Cameras. Also, links about quitting smoking, links to interesting places (Japan, Australia, a shopping mall, the Phillipines ... ) and links that didn't fit anywhere else (Calculus Haters, Overcoming Shyness, Alpha Phi Omega, the weather ... ).
