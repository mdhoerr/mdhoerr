ESTJ Home Pages  
Brian T Mayer on the Web  
(Added 13 Oct 1999)  
The left side of the screen allows you to click through photos, while the right side provides information and links on various topics. The menu is very simple: About, Resume, Portfolio, Email. Clicking on a menu item does not take you off the page, but merely replaces the text on the right side with the new information. When a new page is opened, it is smaller, so you see at once that you have left the site, but you still have Mayer's page available. I was not able to get lost on this site.  
James Bruce Daley's Home Page   
(added Fall 1998)  
is a resume. The home page has a large black and white photo of Mr. Daley, a two sentence summary of his goals and qualifications, and a set of links for more information on other topics. These topics include a description of his Myers-Briggs type, and a list of Frequently Asked Questions. The navigation is well thought out and consistent. I found interesting that his links are the same color as his text before you click them, and change color only have you have visited the link. Since the layout makes it very obvious what is a link, the system works very well.
