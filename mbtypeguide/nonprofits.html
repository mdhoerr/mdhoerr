Paladin Associates, Inc. (updated 26 Sep 1999) 
"was formed as a non-profit organization for the purpose of promoting the benefits of the Myers-Briggs Type Indicator. Through affordable personal consulting and through business related seminars we hope to provide those involved with a solid understanding of themselves and those around them." They have a unique way to get a quick "take" on your type. Instead of answering pages of questions, go the Famous Person's Types page, and click on the picture of famous person you think you are like, and read the type description. Not really precise, but I think it would be a fun introduction to type for some people. They now also offer the MBTI速 instrument online, both the G and the K versions, for $36. You provide your credit card info online over a secure link. They also state: "As part of our non-profit structure we provide free consultation to minority individuals or groups and will provide some financial aid where sanctioned." 
George A. Smathers Libraries (added 31 May 1999) 
The University of Florida's George A. Smathers Libraries has a Special Collections page. If you look under Manuscripts, then General Manuscripts, you find a link to a page called Isabel Briggs Myers Papers, which lists original manuscripts and correspondence of Isabel Briggs Myers archived at the University libraries, as well as some material by her mother, Katherine Cook Briggs. The listing of material available is fascinating.

The APT Great Lakes Regional Conference conference, called "Unlocking the Mysteries of Personality," will be held September 22 - 24, 2000 in Lisle, Illinois.
