Past Link Reviews

New Group for S-preference (added 14 Jun 2000)
Typecast is an Egroup started 2 June 2000 "primarily for those with a sensing preference. However all types are welcome to join. An easygoing and relaxed atmosphere like the former psych-types group is encouraged". As of this date, it had 23 members and 79 posts.

Myers-Briggs Excite Club (added 22 Mar 2000)
Myers Briggs Community is an Excite Club which currently has 12 members. According to the the person who started the club, "This site is for the different MBTI personality types to get to know each other, learn to get along together and for persons of the same MBTI type to share common interests." One of the current topics: What are your views on obtaining a Zen like state?

Yahoo Club for Type Interactions (added 1 Mar 2000)
Started on 16 December 1999, MBTI Type Interactions was created in order to more closely examine how Meyers-Briggs types get along with one another. Do opposite types attract? Or, as has been noted by some nowadays, has that age-old clich√© been disproven?

Yahoo Club for NT Temperament (added 1 Mar 2000)
Started on 30 September 1999, NT Open is being provided to facilitate concentration of the NT mbti temperament. The discussion topics are open and the club is not limited to NT temperaments only.

Yahoo Personality Club (added 23 Feb 2000)
Describing itself as the "Place to meet people with your personality type...", the PersonalityClub, founded in June 1999 has 27 members. Recent topics have been Romantic Matches and Pet Types.

A Yahoo Club for each Type (added 3 Nov 1999) 
Michael S. Smith has a page with a link to the Yahoo Club for each type. These include Yahoo Clubs which he started himself for several types that didn't have one before.  

MBTI Discussion List (added 22 Sep 1999) 
This is a moderated group open to all types and temperaments which was "created on December 9, 1998 for the purpose of discussing the different types and temperaments found in the Myers Briggs Type Indicator and David Keirsey's Please Understand Me.  It is to be a broad based discussion group in which anything concerning the MBTI can be discussed whether it be relationships, intelligence,  communication styles etc." It is a very active egroup, with a minimum of 150 messages per month over the past several months, and sometimes many more. It is moderated by Reggie Chan and Tim Miller.  

On the Border (added 16 Aug 1999) 
Not sure of one of your preference letters, and want to talk about it? MBTI Border Zone is a new type-related e-group for people who are on the borderline: "those of us who score very evenly in one or more areas and who are trying to determine which side of the line best suits us."  

French MBTI EGroup (added 12 Jul 1999, updated 10 May 2000) 
As of 10 May 2000, Egroups no longer listed this egroup. votretype is an egroups email group that is in French. Not much activity as yet, but it was just started last December.  

A Type List for S-types (added 28 Jun 1999 updated 14 Jun 2000)
As of at least 3 June 2000, pSych-Types, which was started for people with the S preference, was closed.
The Psych-Type egroup is an MB related list with an S focus. It is described as: "a list for people interested in talking about psychological types (Jung's theory, Myers-Briggs, etc). It is an all-type-inclusive list focusing on the sensing preference more than on the intuitive preference. Since there are currently several type lists which discuss MBTI and personality type theory (all of which have a predominantly iNtuitive membership and appeal), it would be interesting to discuss topics in a way that might be appealing to people who prefer Sensing." Although it just started in June, it already has 30 members and 80 posts on several subjects.  

Talkway (added 17 May 1999)  
is a site that appears to put discussion groups in a web page based form. Go to the Study of Personality page to view and post to what looks like alt.psychology.personality to me.  

Personality Type Forums (added 1 Mar 1999)  
Doug Dean, whose site on Learning Styles I reviewed last year (Net Links > teaching) has added 17 Personality Type forums, one for each personality type, and one for General Discussion. If you post to the forum for your type, let me know!
