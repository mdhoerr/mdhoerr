Web-Based Career Planning Course (added 26 Apr 2000) 
Architect or Astronaut? is an online course that takes you through the steps of determining what your interests are, identifying jobs that may meet your interests, and then communicating with people in those jobs. The first step, especially, includes links to Myers-Briggs™ resources, as well as other interest surveys online. It was developed for EDTEC 572, a course of the College of Education at San Diego State University. EDTEC is about designing and delivering instructor-led lessons and workshops that are enhanced by technology, especially intranets and the Internet.

CPP Develops Career Site (added 6 Oct 1999) 
Consulting Psychologists Press, Inc., publisher of the MBTI® instrument, has developed a career change site called CareerHub, which provides some free resources as well as a Career Change Package for about $25. The Career Change Package consists of five on-line tools, to help you sort through various aspects of deciding upon and implementing a career change. 

Blind Spots, and Career Ideas (added 7 Jun 1999) 
Here's another set of profiles to add to your lists. Dr. Hall's Personality Type Descriptions also include career ideas for each type, and points out possible blind spots. One INTP blind spot that I share: INTPs may become so fixated on a tiny point of imprecision that they lose sight of the larger system. Who, me? 

Outplacement Use of the MBTI® instrument (added 31 May 1999) 
Ford R. Myers is a Business Coach and Career Consultant. He has several articles on-line, including "Use of The Myers-Briggs Type Indicator in Outplacement" about the appropriate use of the MBTI® instrument in outplacement. He thinks it tends to used too late in the process, and not in enough depth. 

Networking and Introverts (added 24 May 1999) 
CareerLab provides various services for job seekers, including administering the MBTI® instrument (not online, though). Currently, they have an article on networking for introverts. An introvert shares his experience with networking to find another position. 

Dept of Interior has Career Site (added 11 Jan 1999) 
The Department of the Interior has a Career Manager site that includes a list of jobs sorted according to the types they appeal to. As the site says: "The lists are just another tool to give you ideas about careers and jobs you might enjoy. Use the lists as tool not a box!" You might also want to take another look at Damian Conway's site elsewhere on this page.

Database on Career Choice and Type (added Fall 1998, updated 19 Apr 2000) 
As of April 2000, this database is down for upgrading. It's been too popular!
Damian Conway maintains a site, Myers Briggs Personality Type and Career Choice, based on a fascinating concept. People provide information to the database that includes type, information on jobs they have had, and how satisfied they were. I searched on INTP alone (you can also enter other criteria) and got a list of 13 jobs INTP's had entered into the database, ranging from computer technician to sales rep, with a description of how satisfied they were.
