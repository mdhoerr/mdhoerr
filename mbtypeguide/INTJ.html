Groups, Clubs, Lists, WebRings ...  
Yahoo INTJ Club (added 27 Oct 1999)  
This club, called INTJ MBTI type talk, was started April 8, 1999. The founder states: "This community is being provided to facilitate concentration of the INTJ mbti type. The discussion topics are open and the club is not limited to INTJ types only."  
INTJ WebRing   
(added 12 Apr 1999)   
I found the links to the Yahoo Clubs above at the Psychological Type Discussion Forum, which is a member of the INTJ WebRing. You can see the home pages of several INTJs, including Lucrezia, a 16th century duchess.

INTJ-List@OneList.com  (added 19 Jan 2000) 
Started in July 1999, this list now has 177 members. The link above takes you to the home page for the list, where you can read the charter for the list, and see some recommended reading. However, you must first join the list before you can read messages or look at the recommended links section.

INTJ-list (unmoderated) (added 1 Mar 2000) 
Started sometime in December 1997, this is the website for INTJ-list at intj.org, which has an UNMODERATED electronic mailing list for people of INTJ personality type.

INTJ-open email list (moderated) (added 8 Mar 2000) 
This is the home page for a moderated intj list. The moderator's aim for this list is "to provide a place for the INTJ and anyone interested in INTJ's to discuss personality type and how it affects our way of dealing with the world." Two people recommended this list to me. One of them says: There are enough people posting regularly to make it interesting and the content level is good, partly threads on subjects and partly chat. 
intj-c@egroups.com (this is not a link) (added 22 Mar 2000) 
This is the oldest running INTJ group continuously on the web. Membership is closed to 50 members, but individuals are welcome to apply to the email address: intj-c@egroups.com. Submit a short paragraph on any topic of your choosing. What your MBTIÂ® type is and how you determined it. What books on type have you read. This groups focuses on rational discussion. the magisterium
(added 12 July 2000)
This site blows my theory that all INTJ homepages are just lists of annotated links. The INTJ type has introverted Intuition dominant (the other with Ni dominant is INFJ) and it really shows on this site. The second opening quote is from Einstein, saying that the most beautiful emotion is the mystical. The site is a collection of original writings, poems and photography, mostly organized around his philsophy of life.

Joe Kissell's Home Page   
(added 7 June 1999)  
Practically the whole site appears to be a long annotated list of bookmarks on one page. Although it takes a while to load for that reason, you can start reading it before it is finished. Having most of his site on one page makes it very easy to search his page using your built-in browser search-in-page. Besides his long home page, there are links to other pages, such as what appears quite a few books he has read, his music collection, and contradictions (I loved this one: things I believe and their opposites, which I also believe.)
