ENTP Home Pages 
Richard Weber's Home Page  
(added 23 Feb 2000)  
The home page of this site includes a bulleted list of described links to the other pages in his site. Although the bottom of the page indicates the site was last updated in May 1997, looking through the site turns up the text of a talk he gave in October 1999, as well as links for courses he teaches from Winter of 1999. There is no particular order I could find to the list: items seem to be added as he thinks of them, and he doesn't bother to update old links, except those that refer to his class. There is virtually no personal data - almost all the links appear to be related to his intellectual interests.
Hal Dendurent, Catalyst   
(added Fall 1998)  
He claims on his web page that his attitude is "let's talk". He put together the Beer-Drinking questionnaire to determine Myers-Briggs type. I guess he means it - he participates in three listservs: psyche-type, ENFP, and ENTP. His term for a group of ENTP's is "a froth of ENTP's."  
Bob Carpenter's Homepage   
(added 19 Apr 1999)   
The home page is like an internet "business card": it is a very sparse page with a photo, links to info on his current job and company, and contact information. At the bottom of the page is the line of home site categories: Home, Schedule, Projects, Publications, CV and Personal. Although each page is simple and straightforward, the text is very dense with links. There is no "links page" per se: every link is within the context of information on a page. His MB info is a link from the Personal page where he states that: "... despite my scepticism concerning most psychological testing, this one's intentionally not trying to be too fine-grained, and is pretty damn accurate."
