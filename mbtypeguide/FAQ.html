Where can I find a copy of the test and the meanings? 
If you would like a description of each of the sixteen types, my favorite site on the web is at TypeLogic. (Link review: Net Links > companies)
A copy of the indicator (it is not technically a test, since there are no right or wrong answers) is a little more complicated. The official Myers-Briggs Type Indicator ® instrument can only be obtained and administered by someone certified to do so. It is not free. There are several sites where you can pay and take it online. (Net Links > tests) This page also contains a list of several sites that provide online questionnaires based at least in part on Myers-Briggs™ theory, and where you get the same four letter type description. Most of these are free.

Where can I order copies of the MBTI® instrument?
The official Myers-Briggs Type Indicator ® instrument can only be obtained by those who are certified to administer it (see exception for students). If you are qualified, you can get it from Consulting Psychologists Press (CPP) in the United States. My link review with their url is at Net Links > companies CPP. According to the CPP site:
The Myers-Briggs Type Indicator is a "B" level instrument which requires that the user has completed a course in psychological tests and measurement at an accredited university or college. This requirement is not satisfied by a course in statistics, research, or test design.
There is one other way to satisfy the course requirement. CPP recognizes six organizations that give qualifying workshops for the Myers-Briggs Type Indicator. An academic degree is a prerequisite to attending, and they usually last four to five days. Those who successfully complete one of these programs are recommended to us as having training equivalent to a testing course, and enable the participant to purchase "B" level MBTI® materials from Consulting Psychologists Press.

Where can I order copies of the MBTI® Instrument - Student Exception?
Previously, in my FAQ, I had written that you cannot get a copy of the MBTI® instrument unless you are qualified to administer it. Since then, I have found that there is exception if you are a student who needs it for a class project and you meet the requirements listed below. Even then, you are not allowed to include a copy of the MBTI® instrument as part of your thesis, dissertation or project. are exceptions to this general rule. According to Consulting Psychologists Press, Inc., (see my link review) who are the exclusive publisher of the MBTI® instrument:
"If you will be ordering as a student, you do not need to meet the qualification requirements yourself but you must fill out our qualification form and have it co-signed by your advising professor, and submit it with your first order. Be sure to check the box on the qualification form that says "I am a student. My professor has signed below." A qualification form can be printed from our web site, and then faxed or mailed with your initial order."

I am qualified to administer the MBTI® instrument. Do I need any other kind of certification to present in workshops, etc.? If so, what is involved in the certification?
To get the answer, I emailed Consulting Psychologists Press, Inc., (see my link review) who are the exclusive publisher of the MBTI® instrument. Their answer:
"MBTI is B level material, so if you already meet B level requirements, no further training is needed."
Some MBTI® qualifying organizations have workshops to qualify you in using their methodology in presenting MBTI® information. You may want to check out the websites of the six organizations that provide qualifying workshops. The list and their urls can be found on my site in the righthand column of my Net Links > tests page.

Can you provide information about Myers and Briggs, including their qualifications? 
For a good overview of who Isabel Briggs Myers and Katherine Briggs were, and how they developed the MBTI® instrument, check out the Isabel Briggs Myers Papers at the University of Florida George A. Smathers Libraries Special Collections. My link review of this site is in Net Links > Nonprofits.
