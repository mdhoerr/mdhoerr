Jennifer Pearson's Web Site   
(added 22 Feb 1999)  
This is definitely a web site, not a home page. She uses frames with a very clear index on the left and a simple but attractive bordered section for text on the right. As you go through the index, each topic has its own format - one has frames of its own, one opens a new window, one has its own background scheme. Her home page gives the facts: birth date, place, name, etc. and why she's on the web: for faith and family. She has her testimony, wedding pictures, and (almost) natural childbirth story.

Welcome to Malia's Website
(added 5 July 2000)
Last week I listed Malia's site as an ENFP home page (see my original link review in the following paragraph). Since then, she has emailed me that after further reflection and research, she has decided that her type is really ISFP. This set me to thinking about the two types. Both include Fi, and both have an extraverted perceiving function. Extraverted N and extraverted S can be very hard to distinguish, with Se often being mistaken for Ne (the Temperament Research Institute has an article on this very topic called How to tell iNtuiting from extraverted Sensing). The other question was whether Fi was dominant (implying the introverted orientation) or auxiliary (implying the extroverted orientation).
Under the title of the site are what look like two banner ads, one for The Cure and one for Radiohead, but they turn out to be links to her pages devoted to these bands. Under that is a search box link to Amazon. Only then do you get to a table of contents done up to look like a movie marquee. Her pages include film, books, an art gallery of her own drawings that have been scanned, a photo gallery with pictures of friends and family, and an About Me page that has her type. The most ENFP - like part of her site is her description of her life goals on the About Me page. She wants to "to travel the world, truly fall in love, experience all I can, enjoy everything good that life has to offer, do something to help the earth" and that's only the first five things.
