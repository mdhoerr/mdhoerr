Past Link Reviews

Bulletin of Psychological Type (added Fall 1998) 
"The Bulletin of Psychological Type is published eight times per year for APT members around the world. The Bulletin reports news, helpful articles, reviews, and comments about APT and psychological type. Regular features include articles by the APT's interest area consultants and book reviews." There are several good articles on-line, but they don't seem to be updated very frequently, if at all. 

The Center for Highly Sensitive People (added 12 July 2000) 
provides articles and resources for "Intuitive introverts and Highly Sensitive Persons dream about creative, spiritual, metaphysical and holistic health lifestyles." A member of the INFP ring, the site is not specifically focussed on that type but the counselor who runs the site, Thomas Eldridge, identifies himself as an INFP, and the site has a definite NF flavor. Many articles contain stories about an individual's experience of spiritual or personal growth.

Energy Medicine Online (added 10 May 1999)  
is a bimonthly magazine which aims to provide "the latest news in primary healthcare, while creating a forum to explore complimentary ways in which to heal our lives." The magazine includes a regular feature called "Appreciating Differences" written by Jack Falt, a trained facilitator of the Myers-Briggs Type Indicator® instrument and True Colors, who also has his own web site: Jack Falt - Appreciating Differences, with more resources and articles. 

The Enneagram and the MBTI®: an electronic journal on the relationship between the two personality typing systems (added Fall 1998)
Read this journal. The Myers-Briggs™ information here alone is worth it, and if you're also interested in the Enneagram (I'm not) it's an added bonus. The first issue included works submitted by various artists, requesting reader feedback on type. This second issue has the same thing with creative writing samples. There are research articles, and a bulletin board with several active threads. 

InfoWorld Publication (added 8 Dec 1999)
InfoWorld Electric publishes Women in Technology International (WITI) FastTrack. The November 30, 1999 issue contained the article: Skill Set: Taking your brand to market by Kate McLucas, which discussed using the MBTI® instrument to provide supplementary insight into your strengths and weaknesses. She provided two references for more info on MBTI® instrument, and this site was one of them - the other was the CAPT site  (link review at Net Links > nonprofits), as well as Business Development Testing, Inc. (link review at Net Links > companies) as an online source for taking the MBTI® instrument. 

Islands in the Clickstream column (added Fall 1998)
Richard Thieme is a techno-philosopher with a web site called ThiemeWorks - The Wave of the Future. Sometimes, he incorporates type comments in his articles. He's an ENFP, and in his article, a wild-eyed dreamer talks to himself late at night, he uses an interview format to discuss constructing reality, Myers-Briggs™, and the future when "what we call nations will be tribal identities or neighborhoods." He has an interesting exchange about "networking" from the NF and STJ points of view. 

NF Journal  (added Fall 1998) 
This journal is available as a paper subscription for $20/year or as an electronic text only subscription for $10/year for 12 monthly issues. To view a sample text only issue click here. "The personal accounts of NFs, worldwide, comprise a good portion of each monthly issue. Short and feature-length articles are written by NFs, about their personal experiences, the challenges and joys of being who they are, their life journeys, and the depths of their extraordinary ventures in their relationships and into their inner worlds."  

Personality and Individual Differences (added 5 July 2000)
is the Official Journal of the International Society for the Study of Individual Differences (ISSID), is available online as well for a subscription fee. Although I haven't read any Myers-Briggs™ related articles (the free sample didn't happen to have any), when browsing throught key words and the archives, I found occassional Myers-Briggs™ related articles, such as "A hierarchical structural analysis of perfectionism and its relation to other personality characteristics."

Personality Plus (added 15 Sep 1999) 
This is the online publication of the Personality Institute of the Southern Tier, which was "founded to explore the diversity and dynamics of personality, as identified by the Myers Briggs Type Indicator, and its role in the positive development of human potential". There is a great deal of information here, ranging from one of the best summaries I've seen on the history and development of the MBTI® instrument (Myers-Briggs Type Indicator: A Research Report) to type-related financial advice, poetry and more. 

Speculations (added 11 Nov 1999) 
is a print magazine for Science Fiction / Fantasy / Horror writers that has a web site. One of their retired topics, Personality Types, was a discussion on how personality affects the writing process. Although it goes rather far afield, into name analysis and horoscopes as well as MB type, you might want to check it out if you're a writer. 

TypeWorkS® (updated 12 Jan 2000) 
"TypeWorkS® is an eight-page subscription newsletter published six times a year. The newsletter presents practical applications and background information to those using psychological type and the Myers-Briggs Type Indicator® in organization settings." They used to have several articles on line, which weren't updated. Now, you can find an excerpt from an article from each issue. Very good articles. Since April of 1999 TypeWorkS has partnered with Otto Kroeger Associates of Fairfax VA, a leading provider of Qualifying Programs and other programs and tools for type practitioners.

Psychological Types, Chapter X, by Jung (added 25 Aug 1999) 
The MBTI® instrument is based on Jungian types. So how about a look at the chapter Jung wrote that describes them? Read Jung's descriptions of type on-line at Classics in the History of Psychology site.
