Articles I Have Read

Paul Tulenko, SMALL BUSINESS / Looking to Cut a Path? Here Are Some Tools To Help Clear the Way. , Newsday, 04-30-2000, pp 14. (added 3 May 2000)
This article describes various career planning resources that are available to those who are looking to start a new business, change careers, or just discover their potential. Several tests are discussed, including the Myers-Briggs Type Indicator® instrument.

Mary Carmen Cupito, Professional topics "Hire" education: lessons for IT execs. , Health Management Technology, 07-01-1998 (added 26 Apr 2000)
This describes some of the things covered at the College of Healthcare Information Management Executives' (CHIME) May 1998 seminar on IT management. One speaker suggested that IS staff members - typically introverts who are not good at dealing with conflict - may benefit from conflict resolution training. He suggested using Myers-Briggs™ type, while another speaker had a simpler scheme that divided people into four types: analytical, amiable, driver and expressive.

Author not available, JOHN DITIBERIO ST. LOUIS U. PROFESSOR. , St. Louis Post-Dispatch, 02-05-2000, pp 17. (added 19 Apr 2000)
This is the obituary of John DiTiberio, who died Thursday (Feb. 3, 2000). Professor DiTiberio was the author of several type-related books, including Writing and Personality: Finding Your Voice, Your Style, Your Way, with G.H. Jensen.

Anne O'Connor; Staff Writer, `Changing a police culture'. , Minneapolis Star Tribune, 03-17-1996, pp 01B. (added 12 Apr 2000)
This article provides interesting insights into a new chief of police, called in to make the police department more responsive to the citizenry. New Minneapolis Police Chief Robert Olson started out by letting his inner circle of deputy chiefs and administrators know his personality type - ENTP. The article describes how different people - in the department, in the city - react to him, and relates it to his type, from time to time. For example, about three months into the job, "his inner circle bought him a wristwatch - something he's never worn."

Ellen Roseman, High-tech is giving introverts advantage. , The Toronto Star, 02-28-2000. (added 5 Apr 2000)
This business columnist talks about type, the results of her MBTI® instrument (she's an INTP), and problems and advantages introverts have in business. The article is also available for free on the web at the Toronto Star site here.

Author not available, LEGISLATIVE BRANCH APPROPS:ROBERT F. BENNETT. , Congressional Testimony, 03-21-2000. (added 29 Mar 2000)
The budget request of the Office of the Secretary of the Senate for Fiscal Year 2001 includes a report from the Joint Office of Education and Training, which provides employee training and development opportunities for all Senate staff. The professional training branch, which is one of the three branches of the Joint Office, provides courses in areas including: management and leadership development, human resource issues and staff benefits, legislative and staff information, new staff and intern information. Topics include Myers- Briggs Type Indicator® instrument.

The Tampa Tribune, 03-09-2000, pp 1. AMY SCHERZER of The Tampa Tribune (added 15 Mar 2000)
This article describes an 18-week program, in which students spend one morning a week observing hospital careers by visiting Tampa General Hospital. Students who stuck with the programs were able to continue with an "internship" in the department of their choice. Among the many things the students did are mentioned becoming certified in CPR, watching brain surgery, and taking a Myers-Briggs™ Personality Test [sic - it is incorrect to call it a test].

Author not available, Building up confidence. , The Evening Post (Wellington, New Zealand), 01-26-2000, pp 28. (added 9 Feb 2000)
Luke and Kaye Paardekooper provide programmes for corporate events and team building that have included helicoptering participants to the top of Mt Robert in the Nelson Lakes National Park business. The MBTI® instrument is one of the tools they use when putting together their programmes.

Mark Schlabach, Staff, Playing Quarterback: It's a Rush: AN IN-DEPTH LOOK AT THE LIFE OF AN NFL QB: Leaf just had the wrong 'wiring' Pro Football: Special Section. , The Atlanta Journal and Constitution, 09-05-1999, pp G9. (added 2 Feb 2000) 
Niednagel of The Brain Institute (Net Links > companies) recommended that the San Diego Chargers not sign rookie Washington State quarterback Ryan Leaf, because of his brain type. They chose him anyway, and gave him a huge signing bonus. Leaf then went on to have a terrible rookie season, ending it on the bench.

Amy Argetsinger Washington Post Staff Writer, Test of Character; U.S. Naval Academy Analyzes Personality Types to Slow Dropout Rate, The Washington Post, 10-25-1999, pp B01 (added 26 Jan 2000)
The Naval Academy is starting to look at how type can help them identify students at risk for dropping out. There is controvery about whether and to what extent type should be used. As the article shows, although the most common type among Academy freshmen is ISTJ, one officer found that his best freshman student was an ISFP. 

Thomas E. Powers, Profiles of Dropouts, The Washington Post, 11-04-1999, pp A34 (added 26 Jan 2000)
In this letter to the editor written in response to the above article, Powers, the former associate dean of faculty and academic programs at one of the war colleges (the Industrial College of the Armed Forces), points out, correctly, that Myers-Briggs™ type has nothing to do with character, and worries about stereotyping.
