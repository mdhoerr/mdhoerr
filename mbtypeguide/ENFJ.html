Groups, Clubs, Lists, WebRings ... 
ENFJ-List (added 22 Sep 1999)  
is a moderated egroup started in December 1998 for "people who think they are ENFJs as described by the Myer-Briggs Type Indicator (or other books/sorters based on the MBTI) who would like to converse with and get to know other ENFJs."  
Yahoo ENFJ Club (added 27 Oct 1999) 
This club, called ENFJ MBTI type talk, was started September 30, 1999. The founder states: "This community is being provided to facilitate concentration of the ENFJ mbti type. The discussion topics are open and the club is not limited to ENFJ types only."

ENFJ Home Pages 

Senni's page
(added 13 Sep 2000)  
This site was so well organized I couldn't get lost even though I don't read Finnish. There's the home page, personal information (including type), pictures, church links, favorite links, read my guestbook, and add to my guest book.

Mark Bourne's extended engagement  
(added 22 Sep 1999)  
His site serves largely "as an extension of his business card and an addendum to his résumé." He describes himself as a writer who actually makes a living at it. His site is organized into Writing vitae, News, Who, me?, Links, and a link to his own Newsgroup.  

Bits and Pieces   
(added 1 Feb 1999)   
This home site has five pages in all (called home, two, three, four and five), and on the home page you can: get news headlines, visit a message board for people caring for children and aging parents at the same time, browse Gerontology links, conduct a Yahoo search, go to her list of bookmarks, send her email, read the quote of the week, look up a word in a dictionary or a thesaurus, find out her type (including her scores on each preference), browse a list of interesting and cool links, find out when the page was last updated, and view a clock showing you how long you have been on her site. And that's just the home page. But you won't find out her name is Sunshine Red or Ellen Gray Robinson until you get to the middle of the page. Hardly any concrete information here: you won't find out where or when she was born, what her husband's name is, whether she has children, the color of her hair or eyes, or what her house (or apartment) looks like, although some of that is on other pages.  

Martha Beth Lewis' Home Page   
(added 10 May 1999)   
There is a text version available, and the categories are Piano, Needlework, Chocolate, Sailing, Cool Links and Family. The first page then includes a thank you to those who helped her with the site, and to those who recommended the site for awards follows, then her email address. Each category leads to another "home page" on that topic (except Cool Links). Her main interest in type is related to Learning and Teaching Styles, and she has an interesting treatment of the relationship of type to teaching concerns.
