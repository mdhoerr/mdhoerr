Past Link Reviews

Gore and Bush Get Typed (added 28 October 2000)
In Profiles in Personality, Emily Yoffe at Slate gives us her own take on the types of Gore and Bush. Gore is supposed to be INTP and Bush is ESTP. Take a look at the article, and see if you agree.

On-line Course on Personality Type (added 27 Septemer 2000)
Management Centre Europe has an online course introducing personality type. This is not just a quick description of what the letters mean (I, E, N, S, T, F, P, J), although it does include that. It's a step by step introduction to the functions and type, including some exercises. If you've taken one of the sorters based on the MBTI, or been told you are an (fill in the four letter type) and you want to know what it means, this is a good place to start.

WikiWiki Pages on Myers Briggs™ type (added 27 Septemer 2000)
A WikiWiki site provides pages that you can edit online. You can create a page, modify a page, and links are added automatically. It's a very confusing setup - there is truly no "site map" to a WikiWiki site. Anyway, there is a WikiWiki page on Myers Briggs, which includes lots of comments and links to other pages and resources on the topic.

Type Introduction Uses Kirk, Straker (added 26 July 2000)
Myers-Briggs Personality Typing, from the SHADO site, has a decent introduction to type functions, and links to the usual type descriptions that became TypeLogic. Interestingly, it uses the examples of Kirk from Star Trek and Straker from ?? to illustrate ESTJ and ISTJ differences.

C. G. Jung Site Changes Web Address (added 26 July 2000)
The C.G. Jung Page has changed its url from http://www.cgjung.com/ to http://www.cgjungpage.org/homepage.html. It "was founded in 1995 to encourage new psychological ideas and conversationsabout what it means to be human in our time and place..." Myers-Briggs™ type theory is based in part of Jung's theories, and so there are some Myers-Briggs™ related resources here as well, which I've referenced on my Research Net Links page. They also have a link to this site on one of their links pages.

What's Your Money Personality? (added 12 July 2000) 
Goodstewardship.com wants to help you make the most of your time, talent and treasure. They also relate your money management style to your Myers-Briggs type. Determine your type by reading the description of each of the preference pairs and selecting one. Then you get a description of how your type is likely to deal with money. When it comes to money, this site calls an INTP a "Planner", which despite my P seems to fit pretty well.

US Folk Singer David Roth Gives His Type (added 5 July 2000)
This is an interview from December 1999 with singer / songwriter David Roth. One of the interview questions was about his type. The interviewer incorrectly called it a test, which led to some confusion on Roth's part, who clearly knows it is *not* a test. The type he remembers coming up with was INFP.

Introversion, Extraversion, and Cyberversion? (added 21 June 2000)
Introverts get their energy from looking within. Extraverts get their energy from interacting in the real world. What about folks that get their energy from interacting on the internet. Cyberversion anyone? Fellow INTP Laura discusses WHY THERE SHOULD BE 24 TYPES. By the way, I still come out INTP, not CNTP.

Sports Training Programs by Type (added 14 Jun 2000)
Apparently based on Niednagel's work on the relationship between sports performance and type, Sport Consulting Team (SCT) provides coaching programs based on "Action Type ®." At least I think so - the site is in Dutch, and I could read some of it because it is close to German. There is the usual sixteen cell type table, which takes you to information for each type.

Home Pages by MBTI® instrument Type and Enneagram Type (added 14 Jun 2000)
Rodney has put together a list of home pages of people of each MBTI® instrument type, which also includes enneagram information, at his MBTI & Enneagram personality types page. There is a link on this page to one of the online MBTI® instrument-based questionnaires and to an enneagram questionnaire, so you can determine your type and your enneagram if you don't already know it. So far, he has homepages and enneagram information for ISTJ, ISTP, INTP, INTJ and INFP. Email him if you want your page listed too.

Singer-Loomis: a Different Take on Typology (added 7 Jun 2000)
Team Solutions has a good description of a type inventory that has a different interpretation of Jung's types. The Singer-Loomis Type Deployment Inventory attempts to measure each type function separately, instead of determining which of each function pair we most prefer.

Personality Type as Middleware (added 31 May 2000)
Chris Lofting has very dense site, Dichotomies, that describes how we use certain filters, which he calls "middleware" to impose on ourselves a reality that is a mix of what is outside our mind and what is inside our mind, and to create meaning. The I Ching is one such "middleware" filter. The Myers-Briggs personality type is another.

Defensive Communication, Reactive Behavior (added 17 May 2000)
In Achilles Tendencies: Exploring Human Frailty and Personality Type, Roger E. Bissell suggests that TJ, TP, FJ and FP types each have weaknesses that can lead to a breakdown in communication in certain ways. For example, a TP is more likely than an FJ to go into "computer" mode when communication gets defensive, while the FJ is more likely to become placating. Each of these four categories also have more likely ways of expressing reactive behavior. A TJ is more likely to become a "persecutor" while an FP is more likely to "walk out." An insightful article drawing on distinctions I have not seen used this way before.

Bookstore Column About Communication, Type (added 17 May 2000)
In Warp and Woof Article It's Not Theoretically Possible, an example of failed communication gets online bookstore proprietor John Wilbanks of Cardinal Books speculating about the perils of communication personality type and President John Quincy Adams. Question: What did Adams consider his finest achievement? Hint: It had nothing to do with being President. CardinalBook identifies books that should be remembered, either because they're beautiful or they say something important, then obtains electronic rights as necessary, and puts them online.

Recovery by Type (added 10 May 2000)
In her February 1998 article Personality Type and Self-care in Recovery Today, Marthanne Luzader discusses how recovery programs can be tailored to type preferences. Recovery Today is an online newsletter which provides articles about addiction and treatment issues.

Type and Relationships (added 3 May 2000)
TypeLogic (see link review) has a new section to their site that describes how different types get along together. There are 16 different kinds of relationships for each type: Identity, Pal Complement, Contrast, Supplement, Anima, Suitemate, Cohort, Companion, Tribesman, Advisor, Pedagogue, Enigma, Novelty, Neighbor or Counterpart. Click on one type description at the site, and scroll down to find out which other other types stands in which relationship to that type. For example, my Pal relationship is with ENTP, my Advisor relationship is with INFJ and my Pedagogue relationship is with ENFJ. However, to find out what each of these relationships mean, and how the authors derived them, you need to buy A Functional Analysis of Type II: Relationships, which you can download, or have sent to you on diskette. I bought it, and found the analysis very interesting.

Writing by Hand vs Keyboarding (added 26 Apr 2000)
In The Phenomenology of Writing by Hand, Daniel Chandler describes two approaches to writing: Discoverers who use writing to "discover" what they want to say, and Planners who write to record what is already clear in their minds. He discusses how important the media used (writing by hand, typing) to writers that favor each approach. He also relates Planner and Discoverer to various other systems, including Jungian type functions, such as I vs E and T vs F. This article is part of the Media and Communication Studies site (MCS) which is "a British-based gateway to Web resources useful in the academic study of media and communication." This site is down for maintenance on Tuesday mornings (British time).

On-line Action/Suspense Novel with Characters by Type (added 5 Apr 2000)
Ann Hart's action/suspense novel, Midnight Dawn, has an INFP heroine, Dr. Dawn Whisper. The entire novel is available for free online.

Tech Writer Uses Type to Help with Fiction (added 29 Mar 2000)
In an interview in Keystrokes Magazine, technical writer Jerry Kindall describes aspects of technical writing. He is also interested in writing fiction, but he decided he didn't understand enough about people to write good fiction. Part of the research he's doing to help him remedy this deficiency is to study Myers-Briggs type.

LifeExplore Provides Type, Enneagram Resources (added 1 Mar 2000)
LifeExplore: The Personality Supercenter provides information on both systems, including type descriptions, function descriptions, links to other resources, and tables correlating MBTI® instrument type and enneagram number. There is also a forum, and a page where you can post a link to your own site. Cory Caplinger created this website "to introduce personality typing to newcomers, and also as a place for more advanced students of the topic."

Type and Travel (added 9 Feb 2000)
TravelTypes both provides and solicits resources and travel suggestions by type. So far, there are travel related links listed for most types, and a travel page for INFP and ENFP. As people of each type submit travel-related information, Morgan plans to add a travel page for each type.

Matching Owner Temperament with Dog Breed (added 19 Jan 2000)
Dr. Larry Lachman, author of Dogs on the Couch, about non-violent methods for training dogs, has a site called Family Animal. On the site, you can read an excerpt from The Family Animal Veterinarian/Psychologist International Behavioral Newsletter called Matching Your Personality Type With Your Prospective Dog's Personality or Breed. An SP might do well with a Labrador, an NT with a Rottweiler, an NF with a collie and an SJ with a pug. Check out the article for more suggestions, and to find out how he matches dog with owner temperament.  

Presenting Libertarianism to Different Temperaments (added 22 Dec 1999)  
The July 9, 1998 of Liberator Online article Taking On All Types provides tips on how to present Libertarianism to the four different temperaments. 

Gingerbread Nightmare" Bios by Type (added 1 Dec 1999)
This hillbilly-surf-grunge band has a unique twist on their bios page: they don't list anything about the members but their type, and a short type description. Guess you could call them an "IN" band: the three members: INFJ, INFP and INTJ.

Email List Examines Online Experience (added 24 Nov 1999) 
NetDynam is an email list started in October of 1995 by the late Matt Merkley M.D. for the purpose of examining the online experience. Subjects that have occupied the group in the past have included Jungian typology and its effect on online behaviors. Accordingly, there is a chart that shows the type breakdown of many of the list's members. 

Child Type (added 20 Oct 1999) 
At their site Nurture by Nature, built around their book of the same name, the Tiegers include a short quiz to help you determine your child's type. They also include a short description of your child's type, including what builds the self esteem of each type (INTP: "Enduring self-esteem for INTPs comes from being accepted as the private, unique, and ingenious people they are").  

T vs F Getting Ready for the Holidays (added 20 Oct 1999) 
In his article Santa Claus vs. Scrooge, Ray Hawkins, PhD describes how the T-F preference affects how we look at the holidays. He also provides some suggestions for dealing with the differences.  

Shadow Descriptions (added 6 Oct 1999, updated 24 May 2000) 
Update: the site appears to be in the middle of renovation, and the page is not available at this time.
Cedric Dark has a different take on type descriptions. He lists each type, then describes its shadow, and the preferences that need to be developed further by that type.  

Myers-Briggs Proximity Chart (added 6 Oct 1999) 
Robert Munafo has put together a proximity chart of MB types organized such that the each type is surround on all four side by the four most related types. Besides the usual type descriptions, he also has descriptions for three types with one unclear preference: ExTJ, ExTP, xNTP  

Negotiating Styles of Different Types (added 29 Sep 1999) 
Lowell Halverson maintains a Family Law site. In the section on Using Temperament Sorters to Improve Negotiations, the site covers the negotiating strengths and weaknesses of each type, how each type sees perceives itself in negotiating, and how it is perceived by others.  

Personality Parlour (added 22 Sep 1999) 
The Personality Parlour site is another compilation of type and temperament descriptions, with some nice graphics and effects. 

Type and Anorexia (added 15 Sep 1999) 
Can anorexia sometimes be the result of trying to force one's type to conform to familty expectations? In Eating Disorders: Beyond the Persona, from the APT website (Netlinks > nonprofits) Heidi J. Dalzell discusses one situation where an ENFJ woman described trying to make herself into the ISTJ she felt her family wanted her to be.  

Using Type in Romance Novels (added 8 Sep 1999) 
Laurie Campbell describes various ways to use psychology in creating characters for romance novels. In her article, The Psychology of Creating Characters, she discusses birth order, priorities, enneagram and personality scales (MB type). 

Matchmaking by Type (added 8 Sep 1999, updated 15 Mar 2000, 26 Apr 2000) 
26 Mar 2000: Personality, Character, Temperament & Compatibility has moved to a new location. Click the site name to go to the new url.
15 Mar 2000: Psychological Matchmaking discovery springboard is now called Personality, Character, Temperament & Compatibility, but is still at the same URL. There is now a privacy policy noted on the site.
This is a site where you can enter your type, gender, age, geographical location, and a few words about yourself, and get a list of others who are your same type, opposite type, or complementary type (as defined by Keirsey). If you're not sure of one of your preferences, you can also search using X for the preference you're not sure of: for example, you could search on INXP. If you want to get the email address of someone on the list, you first have to add yourself to the list and provide your email.

On Type and Stereotype in IS (added 25 Aug 1999) 
In his InfoWorld column, Bob Lewis discusses how Personality profiling can open employees to new ideas -- or close minds. Besides the article itself, there are a quite a few responses to the article available.   

GenerousNet (added 24 May 1999) 
is a site that provides many ways for people with "generous builds, generous spirits, generous attitudes" to communicate with each other. There are mailing lists, bulletin boards, and an option for posting your profile if you are interested in romance. One of the questions on the profile template asks for your MBTI® instrument type. 

Skater's Camp Includes Type Session (added 19 Apr 1999)  
As of 29 May 2000, this article was no longer available. The USFSA magazine still has articles online.
The United States Figure Skating Association 1998 Camp of Champions concentrated on working with world class coaches, but it included several sessions off the ice, including instruction on Myers Briggs type. The personality and relationship workshops using the Myers-Briggs tests were "to help all dance and pair teams understand their own personality types as well as their partners."  

On-Line Journallers and Type (added 22 Mar 1999)  
Doug Franklin has an on-line journal called Nilknarf. He also has a list of other on-line journallers, sorted by Type. On a recent summary, 227 journallers identified themselves as having an N preference, 75 with an S preference, and 7 had a ? for the S-N dichotomy. The breakdown by F-T was 192 with the F preference, 107 with T, and 10 with ? for F-T dichotomy.  

Mothers and Type (added 8 Feb 1999)  
Ever wonder how your type might affect your parenting style? M.O.M.S. (Mothers of Many Styles) is an organization with a "unique approach to parenting. Using a framework of personality type developed by Carl Jung and Isabel Myers, we help women identify their strengths and use that information to enrich family relationships." In their article "Type Help for Working Mothers", she points out that mothering is stressful in different ways to different types. She discusses ways mothers can balance their other work with their "mothering job."  

Escape from the Cult of Personality Tests (added 25 Jan 1999)  
In this article, Thomas A. Stewart describes a company where people wore color-coded badges to show their personality type. Although I sometimes think it would make things easier for me (I'm not particularly good at judging someone's type until I've known them a matter of months), that sounds like an invasion of privacy to me, as it did to him. He also talks about type being used to screen out job applicants.  

An Army Depot Uses Type (added 25 Jan 1999)  
The Corpus Christi Army Depot (CCAD) is trying to become a High Performing Organization (HPO). "To achieve this level of performance, it is necessary to build a mechanism for change. ... CCAD built this mechanism on a series of interconnected programs that support personal growth and continuous improvement in the organization." Their program includes Myers-Briggs training, Dr. Stephen R. Covey's Seven Habits of Highly Effective People, Honesty, Ethics, Accountability, Respect, Trust & Support (HEARTS) training and HPO training.

Limiting Ourselves with Type (added in 1998)  
Does categorizing people according to type limit them? Scott Arbuthnot, in his article "Type Tools: Mind How You Use Them" warns us that we can use type to limit ourselves.
