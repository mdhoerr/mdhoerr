Past Link Reviews

Type and the Bible (added 21 June 2000)
Ron Duer's page, The M.B.T.I. & the B.I.B.L.E.: A guide for type watchers who are also Bible students is the most in-depth on-line resource I've seen that applies type theory to various aspects of the Bible. Ron Duer describes what he thinks the dominant function, the temperament, and the type is of each of the evangelists. He then backs it up with word counts from the Bible, and other analysis.

Developing Lay Ministries Incorporating Type (added 10 May 2000)
Dr. Lawson, Director of Congregational Ministries with the Brethren Church, conducts The Living Sacrifice Workshop, which "is designed to help [Christian] pastors and churches develop lay ministries in the church based upon spiritual gifts and personality type." In the illustration, Spiritual Gifts are the foundation of ministry. Burdens and Desires affect where the gifts will be used. Personality Type affects how the gifts will be used.

Christianity, Mysticism and Jungian Psychology (added 5 Apr 2000)
Inner Explorations describes itself as "Where Christian mysticism, theology and metaphysics meet Eastern religions, Jungian psychology and a new sense of the earth." In their section on The Jungian-Christian Dialogue, they cover the very real problems between Jungian Psychology and Christianity. Their suggestion seems to be to recognize that the process of individuation is not the same as the prayer life, although the prayer life can be affected by type and the process of individuation. As an orthodox Roman Catholic, (the site draws heavily on Roman Catholic spirituality and tradition) I have some reservations about this site, but it appears to have good and objective information about type (one aspect of Jungian psychology) and spirituality.

Gesher (added 8 Mar 2000)
is the name of a nonprofit organization. According to the site, it "means "bridge" in modern Hebrew, reflecting its founder's desire to bridge the widening gap seen between people's spiritual life and their personal, business, and family life--something we call personal restoration." One aspect of this involves using type. The President of Gesher, Robert Winer, M.D., also uses type professionally "to assist Pharmaceutical Representatives in forming and improving their relationships with physicians. He also acts as a consultant in his areas of expertise as they relate to the legal, ethical, and moral issues." 

Marine Corps Community Services (added 1 Mar 2000)
Scroll to the bottom of the Frequently Asked Questions page for information on Religious Programs. CREDO stand for Chaplains Religious Enrichment Development Operation. It is a series of retreat programs for units, couples, and individuals. One of the three basic programs is the Team Building Retreat, which uses the MBTI® instrument in helping to build unit cohesiveness and increasing cooperation.

Christian vs. Mystic (added 2 Feb 2000)
An INFP who is a born again Christian asks how her NF temperament fits in with her Christian faith. The columnist answers by distinguishing between using one's preferences to make day to day decisions, and using them to come to conclusions about faith.

Teachers of the Craft - Part 3  (added 19 Jan 2000)
provides an introduction to Myers-Briggs™ type to aid Wiccan teachers. The short temperament overview section provides some humorous descriptions of how teachers with different temperaments might approach different Wiccan teaching situations.  

The Discipling Dilemma (added 3 Nov 1999) 
This book by Flavil R. Yeakley, Jr., Editor, Howard W. Norton, Don E. Vinzant and Gene Vinzant is now available online. Chapter Two of the book provides the results of MBTI® studies done on members of the Boston Church of Christ, with that church's permission. The study followed type development among members of that church. It found that members of the Church were reporting a change in type, and that the change converged toward one type: ESFJ.  

Scott and White Pastoral Care (added 29 Sep 1999) 
This site has a lot more than Myers-Briggs™ type info. Their services include Chaplaincy, Clinical Pastoral Education, Pastoral Counseling, Hospice and more. The CPE Program Coordinator, listed in the Team section, leads their students in didactics on the subject of the MBTI® instrument.  

Effective Results (updated 2 August 1999) 
This is a Christian consulting firm that uses the MBTI® instrument to teach individuals what their strengths and opportunity areas are, then uses scripture references to strengthen the individual's Christian testimony and ability to spread the gospel. Their goal is: "Teaching Christians to embrace their diverse personalities to extend the gospel." They provide biblical examples to illustrate the different type preferences, and include a free quarterly online newsletter.  

Leadership U (added 29 Mar 1999) 
is a project of Christian Leadership Ministries, part of Campus Crusade for Christ, International. Their site contains an article by Dr. John H. Stoll, is Executive Director of A.S.K., Inc., a professional counseling and Bible teaching organization, called "The Use of the Myers/Briggs Instrument in Sanctification of Life and Marriage Relationships." He discusses how knowledge of type can help resolve communication problems, and how it can be used to develop one's spiritual life.  

Is MBTI® Type Theory Compatible with Christianity? (added 29 Mar 1999) 
Rev. Ed Hird, Past National Chair of ARM (Anglican Renewal Ministries) Canada, brings up aspects of Myers-Briggs™ type theory that he considers incompatible with Christian beliefs, in his report "Carl Jung, Neo-Gnosticism, & the MBTI." Carl Jung's psychology departs from traditional Christian teaching on many points, and the MBTI® instrument is based on aspects of his psychology. Can the MBTI® instrument be separated from the non Christian aspects of Jungian psychology? 

Cedarville College (added 1 Feb 1999) 
is a Baptist College located in Cedarville Ohio dedicated to providing a Christian education. They provide Counseling Services for their students, including the option to use the Myers-Briggs inventory. "Through the use of the Myers-Briggs Type Inventory (MBTI) we can learn a number of things about people...ourselves and others. That knowledge can help us accept others, and find ways to grow as individuals. In a Christian college setting it has done all of that and more, as we have used it to help students select majors and career fields, work together on various teams, and understand their individual learning styles. In addition it has become an invaluable tool in premarital counseling."  

Institute for Contemporary Spirituality (added Fall 1998) 
This site includes the Brochure for 1996-97. The Institute is part of the University of Scranton, a Jesuit University. Their nine-month intern program, "Enhancement in the Art and Skills of Christian Spirituality," includes about a month on Jungian types. Other programs include "Jungian Type and Personal Growth", which includes taking the MBTI® instrument, and "Understanding Differences Discretely", which uses a new Jungian sorter, The Personality Profiler, to will explore each of the Jungian pairs, extraversion/introversion, sensing/intuition, thinking/feeling, organizing/adapting along with a personal stress indicator.  

APT: Religious and Spiritual Issues (added Fall 1998) 
The Association of Psychological Type maintains an interest area called Religious and Spiritual Issues. There are FAQ's on type and spiritual issues, a list of reading material, and an email address for more information.  

Dard's Daily Devotions Kona Koinonia (added Fall 1998) 
Eight days of devotions. Each day includes a thought on some topic. It reads from the bottom of the page, Day 1, to the top of the page, Day 8. Day 1 includes Dard's thoughts on type. The Rev. Dard Allen is the pastor of Kailua Bay Church, An O'hana of Faith.  

Type, Temperament and Church Life, from TypeWorkS newsletter (added Fall 1998) 
Episcopal priest Lloyd Edwards describes how clergy can use a knowledge of type to prevent any church members from feeling like outcasts. This article, a review of Edwards' book, How We Belong, Fight, and Pray, appeared in the online version of the TypeWorkS newsletter.

Rev. Samual A. Trumbore  (added Fall 1998) 
ENFJ, is a minister of the Unitarian Universalist Fellowship of Charlotte County. In this sermon, he reviews worship styles as discussed by another UU minister, Peter Tufts Richardson, in his book Four Spiritualities: Expressions of Self, Expressions of Spirit: A Psychology of Contemporary Spiritual Choice In the book, the four spiritualities are based on the four function pairs, ST, SF, NT, NF instead of the four temperaments. ST spirituality is the Path of Works, SF is the Path of Devotion, NT is the Path of Unity and NF is the Path of Harmony. I have read the book, and Rev. Trumbore does a good job of describing the spiritualities, and their practical implications for planning and conducting worship services.

I've Read ...

Prayer and Temperament: Different Prayer Forms for Different Personality Types, by Chester P. Michael and Marie C. Norrisey  
This book, written from the Christian tradition, describes four methods of prayer that correlate to the four types: Ignatian prayer - SJ, Augustinian prayer - NF, Franciscan prayer - SP, and Thomistic prayer - NT. According to the authors, the NT prayer form was imposed on clergy until fairly recently, and although they try to avoid it, in my opinion there is still an obvious bias against the NT prayer form in the book.
