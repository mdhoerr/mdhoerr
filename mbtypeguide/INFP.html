Groups, Clubs, Lists, WebRings ...  
Yahoo INFP Club (added 27 Oct 1999) 
This club, called INFP MBTI type talk, was started September 30, 1999. The founder states: "This community is being provided to facilitate concentration of the INFP mbti type. The discussion topics are open and the club is not limited to INFP types only."  
INFP Ramblings (updated 12 Jan 2000)   
The INFP List used to have a webspace called INFP Ramblings, which is no longer available. The INFP profile which used to be on that site is now only available from Elizabeth Buie's Webspace (see INFP Home Pages to the right) at INFP Profile. 
INFP egroup list (added 8 Mar 2000) 
This list, which was recommended by a visitor to Myers-Briggs ® Personality Type on the Web, was started 5 October 1999, and had 618 members as of the date of this review. Over the last three months, the list has had over 1000 posts per month. The list information states: INFPs ONLY!!! ... We can chat about pretty much anything, and maybe we'll even discover some similarities in eachother. I'm really curious to find out! This should be a lot of fun, so please sign up if you're interested. 
INFP webring (added 8 Mar 2000) 
This webring, which was recommended by a visitor to Myers-Briggs ® Personality Type on the Web, currently has 65 member sites. The reason given for the webring is: INFP personalities make up only about 1% of the general population. We are healers and Idealists, we have hope for the future, so we are important to the world. Together we can do more for healing each other and our earth. Besides, with such a small percentage of us, we often feel lonely... we need and can give support for each other.

This site was recommended as an INFP resource on one of my feedback forms. TravelTypes was reviewed in February of this year (see link review). The INFP page includes a description of the INFP travel style, two articles, links to six web pages with INFP travel experiences, and links to lots of books, films, and music selections related to travel. INFP Home Pages  

Main Page
(added 20 Sep 2000)   
This site was recommended as an INFP resource on one of my feedback forms. The simple, mainly text home page is divided into four categories: personal essays mostly about relationships and feelings, an essay and a poem by other authors, something on his type, and some family pictures. You have to do quite a bit of browsing to find out as much as his name, but you get a strong picture of what is important to him. Read his poignant description of his job in What Do I Do? (although I'm not sure whether it refers to his actual job, or to reflections on a job like that).

Thomas E. Ahlswede   
(updated 18 Jan 1999 and 24 May 2000)   
Update: Dr. Hack's Shack is gone from the title, and the link locations have changed.
The only title on the page is "Thomas E. Ahlswede", but if you look up at the name of the window, you find "Dr. Hack's Shack" as part of the title. He also has a page of links to home pages of other people who list their type from the Psych-Type Mailing List. There are home pages listed for 6 of the eight N types, (no INTJ or INFJ), but no homepages for any of the S types.  

Cindy's Ever-Evolving Homepage  
(added Fall 1998, updated 24 May 2000)  
Update: New url.
Starts with a pretty prairie photo, a quote by Ralph Waldo Emerson and a paragraph about finding beauty and attitude. Then a link to more beautiful photos. Then several links, in table format and no particular order, on her interests. The Just for Fun image this week comes from her Prairie Flowers 1998 photo collection. Then favorite movies, favorite CDs, more photos, just keep on scrolling down her page.  

Elizabeth Buie's Webspace   
(added 26 Apr 1999)   
Elizabeth's webspace is clearly and consciously designed for ease of use, and even includes some pages in Italian. In fact, one of her two major pursuits is usability - the other is photography. Her webspace is divided into personal, usability and photo sections, with her Myers-Briggs information linking from her personal section. She also has some comments on INFP profiles in general, including that developed from the INFP mailing list.
